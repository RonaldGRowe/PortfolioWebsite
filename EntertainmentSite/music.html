Jazz

Jazz made its appearance in the U.S. over a 100 years ago in New Orleans, Louisiana. Surprisingly, its roots were founded in Africa and Europe. Jazz uses strong but flexible syncopated rhythm, improvised vocals (both solo and group performances), and a wide variety of harmonies and instrumental techniques. Jazz gained popularity through the performances of early stars like Louis Armstrong, who took the stage by storm and electrified audiences.

Blues

The blues originated in the Deep South's predominantly black communities near the end of the 1900's. Blues music is the result of blending soulful spirituals hymns, slave songs, field hollers, and narrative ballads. The result is a genre that speaks to the hearts of the down- trodden and broken-hearted. Every blues song tells a unique story; a story about life, a story about pain or disappointment, a story about hope, a story about rising above the difficulties of life.

Zydeco

Zydeco evolved from southwestern Louisiana's French-Creole speaking musicians. It is a unique blend of Cajun music, Blues, and . The music is dominated by its fast tempo. In the beginning of zydeco, the music was played mostly by a button or piano accordion, and a washboard. It is a great music to stay moving and dance to, and is a great way to get loose and leave your worries behind. Zydeco is very popular and prevalent in south Louisiana's current music scene.

Swamp Pop

Swamp pop is a musical genre indigenous to the Acadiana region of southern Louisiana and the adjoining section of southeastern Texas. Founded in the late 1950's and early 1960's by teenaged locals, it combines New Orleans-style rhythm and blues, country and western, and traditional French Louisiana musical styles. Swamp Pop is a perfect blend of a little bit of many types of music, and the outcome is an upbeat, energetic, fun music.
